Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen. Normal range for adult males is 13.0-17.0 g/dL and for adult females is 12.0-15.0 g/dL. Low hemoglobin indicates anemia which can cause fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath. High hemoglobin may indicate dehydration or polycythemia.

RBC Count measures the number of red blood cells per cubic millimeter of blood. Normal range is 4.5-5.5 million/cmm for males and 4.0-5.0 million/cmm for females. Low RBC indicates anemia. High RBC may indicate dehydration or bone marrow disorders.

WBC Count measures white blood cells which fight infection. Normal range is 4000-10000 per cmm. High WBC (leukocytosis) often indicates infection, inflammation, or immune disorders. Low WBC (leukopenia) may indicate bone marrow problems or autoimmune conditions.

Neutrophils are the most common white blood cells and first responders to infection. Normal range is 40-80%. High neutrophils suggest bacterial infection. Low neutrophils increase infection risk.

Lymphocytes are white blood cells involved in immune response. Normal range is 20-40%. High lymphocytes suggest viral infection. Low lymphocytes may indicate immune deficiency.

Platelet Count measures cells that help blood clot. Normal range is 150000-400000 per cmm. Low platelets (thrombocytopenia) increases bleeding risk. High platelets may indicate inflammation or iron deficiency.

Glucose measures blood sugar levels. Fasting normal range is 70-99 mg/dL. Prediabetes is 100-125 mg/dL. Diabetes is 126 mg/dL or above. High glucose indicates diabetes or prediabetes. Low glucose indicates hypoglycemia.

HbA1c measures average blood sugar over 3 months. Normal is below 5.7%. Prediabetes is 5.7-6.4%. Diabetes is 6.5% or above. This test is used to diagnose and monitor diabetes management.

Creatinine measures kidney function. Normal range is 0.7-1.2 mg/dL for males and 0.5-1.0 mg/dL for females. High creatinine indicates impaired kidney function. Used alongside eGFR to assess kidney health.

ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) is a liver enzyme. Normal range is 7-56 U/L. High ALT indicates liver damage or disease. Causes include hepatitis, fatty liver, medications, and alcohol use.

AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) is found in liver and heart. Normal range is 10-40 U/L. High AST alongside high ALT strongly suggests liver disease.

Cholesterol total normal is below 200 mg/dL. Borderline high is 200-239 mg/dL. High is 240 mg/dL and above. High cholesterol increases risk of heart disease and stroke.

HDL cholesterol is the good cholesterol. Normal is above 40 mg/dL for males and above 50 mg/dL for females. Higher HDL is protective against heart disease.

LDL cholesterol is the bad cholesterol. Optimal is below 100 mg/dL. High LDL increases risk of arterial plaque and heart disease.

TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) measures thyroid function. Normal range is 0.4-4.0 mIU/mL. High TSH indicates hypothyroidism. Low TSH indicates hyperthyroidism.

Vitamin D normal range is 30-100 ng/mL. Deficiency is below 20 ng/mL. Low vitamin D is linked to bone weakness, immune dysfunction, and depression.

Vitamin B12 normal range is 200-900 pg/mL. Deficiency causes anemia, nerve damage, and cognitive issues. Common in vegetarians and elderly people.

ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate) measures inflammation. Normal for males is 0-15 mm/hr and females 0-20 mm/hr. High ESR indicates inflammation, infection, or autoimmune disease.

CRP (C-Reactive Protein) measures acute inflammation. Normal is below 1 mg/L. High CRP indicates active infection or inflammation in the body.

Uric Acid normal range is 3.5-7.2 mg/dL for males and 2.6-6.0 mg/dL for females. High uric acid causes gout and kidney stones.
